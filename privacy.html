Privacy

Privacy notice.

Last updated July 2026

This notice explains how the current Orgasmaphoria public website handles information. It should be updated whenever accounts, memberships, payments, private content, analytics, or additional services are introduced.

Information you choose to provide

When you use the contact form, you may provide your name, email address, topic, subject, message, and consent confirmation. This information is used to review, route, and respond to your request.

Local website preferences

The site stores your adult-entry confirmation and accessibility preferences in your browser’s local storage. These settings remain on your device until you clear site data or reset the accessibility preferences. They are not used to create a member profile.

Spotify

The Spotify player is not loaded until you choose to load it. Once loaded, Spotify may receive information about your device and visit under Spotify’s own privacy practices. You can instead use the direct Spotify link without loading the embedded player on this site.

Hosting and form processing

The website host may process technical logs needed to deliver and secure the site. Contact form submissions may be processed by the site’s hosting provider so they can be delivered to Orgasmaphoria. The business should retain messages only as long as reasonably needed for the inquiry, business records, safety, or legal obligations.

Payments and accounts

The current public website does not create member accounts, process subscriptions, collect payment card information, or host private member messages. Separate notices and controls will be added before those services are activated.

Your choices

You can avoid loading Spotify, clear local browser storage, or contact Orgasmaphoria to ask about a message you submitted. Requests may require reasonable verification before information is disclosed, corrected, or deleted.

Contact

Use the contact form and choose “Privacy question.”
